South Korea

At age 18, I had the greatest idea to give myself a 19th birthday present after almost a year of working 40 hours at Starbucks, though they claim I was just a part-time employee, and after finally graduating high school during a miserable time around my life. Having just recently received a credit card, I decided to buy myself a plane ticket after clicking on an article about South Korea opening up its borders for tourism. Now I was already familiar with the South Korean culture and language through my Korean friends, and my students (I was an online English tutor for a while).

Buying the plane ticket was the easy part, but the process after was quite difficult due to covid. I had to do at least 1 month of research and ask around this subreddit, Korea Travel.

Entry Process:

  • K-ETA:
    1. To be able to actually enter the country, you would have to apply for an online visa and the process should, at the most, last 3 days depending on your information. After the process is done, you can print the document or download their app to acquire the qr code linked straight to the visa.
  • Apply for a Q-Code:
    1. This is where it got a bit complicated for me. Before South Korea, I was in New York for a week and a half for a family reunion alongside my family friends, and I had an overnight layover in Vancouver (I found mice lodged in between the seats there). So I had to look for a way to get myself tested for COVID, thankfully my uncle is a Doctor, and I had no need to get tested in Vancouver as I was only staying in the airport. Th Q-Code application process for this isn't long, though you do need to acquire a vast amount of information:
      1. Passport Information
      2. Airline Ticket
      3. Vaccine Records (Vaccinated at least 2 times)
      4. COVID Test Results
    2. Since your COVID test results take a while to process, you have to time your test just right before your flight, max 2 days, and hope that you will receive your test results before you reach the border.
  • Book for a COVID Test in South Korea:
      There is a COVID testing center just right outside the Incheon Airport and you can book through here.
